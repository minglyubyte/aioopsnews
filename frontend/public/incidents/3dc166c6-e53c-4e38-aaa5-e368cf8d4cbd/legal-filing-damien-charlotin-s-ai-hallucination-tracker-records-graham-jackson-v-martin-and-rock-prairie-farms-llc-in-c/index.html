Melvin Graham-Jackson, a pro se litigant, filed an appeal with false legal citations in his briefs, citing non-existent legal authorities and unrelated cases. The Court of Appeals identified this as a violation of Wisconsin's appellate rules. Despite being notified of these issues in the respondents' brief, Graham-Jackson continued to use false citations in his reply brief. The court imposed two sanctions: a $500 monetary penalty payable to the defendants to offset their legal expenses, and a requirement for Graham-Jackson to submit an affidavit in any future appeals certifying the accuracy and relevance of his legal citations.
